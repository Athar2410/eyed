Learn About Eye Diseases

Diabetic retinopathy is a complication of diabetes that affects the eyes. It occurs when high blood sugar levels damage the tiny blood vessels in the retina, leading to vision problems and, if untreated, even blindness. Early stages may have no symptoms, making regular eye exams crucial for diabetic patients.

A cataract is a clouding of the eye’s natural lens. It is the most common cause of vision loss in people over age 40. Symptoms include blurry vision, glare, and trouble seeing at night. Cataracts can be corrected with surgery.

Glaucoma is a group of eye diseases that damage the optic nerve, usually due to high eye pressure. It causes gradual vision loss and often has no warning signs. Early detection through eye exams is vital. Treatment can include medication or surgery.
